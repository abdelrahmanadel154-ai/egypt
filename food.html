Food in Egypt

kushari

Kushari (or Koshary) is one of the most famous traditional Egyptian dishes and a beloved street food. It’s a hearty, filling, and inexpensive meal that has become a symbol of Egyptian cuisine. What is Kushari? Kushari is a mix of: Rice Lentils (usually brown lentils) Macaroni or pasta Chickpeas Crispy fried onions on top Tangy tomato sauce flavored with garlic and vinegar Spicy chili sauce (optional) All these ingredients are layered together in one bowl and topped with the special sauces. History Kushari has an interesting origin. It is believed to have been influenced by Indian, Italian, and Middle Eastern cuisines: The rice and lentils combination is similar to Indian khichdi. The use of pasta comes from Italian influence in Egypt in the 19th century. Egyptians added their unique sauces and spices to create the dish we know today. Kushari became popular in the late 1800s and early 1900s as an affordable, nutritious meal for workers and soldiers. Today, it’s eaten by all classes of society and is sold everywhere, from small street carts to big restaurants. Why is it loved? Cheap and filling: You get a big portion for a low price. Vegetarian-friendly: No meat, making it suitable for many diets. Flavorful: The mix of tangy, spicy, and crunchy elements makes every bite interesting. Where to try it? In Egypt, famous kushari restaurants include: Abou Tarek in Cairo (very popular among locals and tourists) Koshary El Tahrir Many street vendors all over the country. Fool

Fool (also spelled Ful or Foul) is another classic and beloved Egyptian dish. It’s made primarily from fava beans and is a staple breakfast food in Egypt and many parts of the Middle East. What is Fool? Main ingredient: Cooked fava beans, slowly simmered until soft. Seasoning: Olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and sometimes cumin. Optional additions: Garlic, tomatoes, onions, chili, tahini, or boiled eggs. It is usually eaten with baladi bread (Egyptian flatbread) and sometimes accompanied by falafel (called taameya in Egypt). Why is Fool popular? Affordable: It’s a cheap, filling dish eaten by everyone. Nutritious: Fava beans are rich in protein, fiber, and iron. Customizable: You can prepare it plain or with many toppings and spices. How is it served? Traditionally in a metal pot called a qidra, often cooked overnight on low heat. Served in bowls with bread for dipping. Famous places to try Fool in Egypt: Felfela in Cairo Zooba (modern Egyptian street food chain) Street carts in every neighborhood, especially in the mornings.
